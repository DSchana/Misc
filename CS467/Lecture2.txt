3 main objectives of security
	- Confidentiality
	- Integrity
	- Avalibility

Confidentiality:
	- Prevent sensitive info from reaching the wrong people

Integrity:
	- Maintain consistence and trustworthiness
	- Guarantee the data cannot be changed by unauthorized people

Avalibility:
	- Ensure timely and reliable access to information

Authentication:
	- Process of determining whether someone or something is who or what they declare to be

Authorization:
	- Process of specifying access rights to resources

SECURITY ATTACKS
	- Any action that compromises the security of information owned by an organization

	- Passive Attack
		- Attemps to learn or make use of information from the system but does not affect system resources
		- Very dificult to detect since they do not invlove any data modification

	- Active Attack
		- Involve some modification of the data
		- Mascarade (pretend to be an online identity to recive data)

		Types:
			- Denial of service
			- Man in the middle
			- Gain access

THREAT AND VULNERABILITY
	- Threat
		- A potential for violation of security, which exists when there is a circumstance, capability, action or event that could breack security and cause harm
		- i.e. A possible danger that might exploit a vulnerability
